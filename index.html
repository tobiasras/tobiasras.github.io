Chess on 3d surface

For this project, I created a chess game where its played on top of cube. The game is built using the 3D JavaScript library Three.js.

Some of the pieces move a little diffrent compared to a normal chess board.

  • Pawns: can move 2 forward on the first move but can also go left and right, down and up. They can attack on the 4 diagonals
  • Bishop, Horse, Rook, Queen: Moves like in a normal chess game

Gamemodes: I have created 2 game modes:

  • Normal play: Like a normal chess game players take turn moving the piece till the king is checkmate
  • Free play: No turns, a player can move the check pieces around without taking turns.

Ingredient Suggester Web App

This web app helps users discover new recipe ideas by suggesting ingredients based on what they already have.

It was built using unsupervised machine learning techniques to cluster over half a million recipes by ingredient similarity.

Behind the scenes, I experimented with models like K-Means, Gaussian Mixture Models, and BIRCH to uncover meaningful patterns in the dataâ€”grouping recipes into categories like desserts, savory dishes, and even tomato-based meals or fruity drinks.

How it works:

To train the ingredient suggestion model, I used the RecipeNLG dataset containing over 2 million recipes. I focused on the cleaned ingredient lists (NER column), removed rare ingredients, and filtered out recipes with fewer than 4 ingredients.

Ingredients were one-hot encoded, resulting in high-dimensional vectors (~200,000 dimensions). To make clustering feasible, I applied dimensionality reduction using Singular Value Decomposition (SVD), reducing the data to 25 components.

I experimented with three clustering models: K-Means, Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM), and BIRCH. Each model was trained on a sample of 500,000 recipes. Clustering quality was evaluated using silhouette scores and visual inspections of ingredient/title word clouds.

The final ingredient suggestion tool uses the 4 best-performing models to assign user-inputted ingredients to a cluster, and then recommends the most frequent ingredients from that cluster.
